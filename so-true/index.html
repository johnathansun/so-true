Musings

from a bard of our time.





































Oh fair Victoria and Johnathan, a pair
Of hearts so bound in love's enduring clasp,
Thou art as sweet as summer's gentle air,
And with thy laughter, fill the world with zest.
In step with each other, ye take long walks,
And in the silence, secrets thou dost share,
With eyes aglow, ye gaze upon the flocks
Of ducks and squirrels, wandering free and fair.
And in the night, when all the world is still,
Thou liest down on "the bean", in peace divine,
With thoughts of love and happiness to fill,
And from each other, deep emotions twine.
Oh blessed couple, let your love remain,
And hold each other's hearts forever strong and fain.






























































In Johnathan's arms, Victoria finds her peace,
A haven safe from all the world's alarms,
With gentle words and tender, loving ease,
She rests her head, protected from all harm.
Together they explore the world around,
And find delight in every simple thing,
From nature's creatures, on the ground,
To conversations that go on and sing.
They share the secrets of their souls so dear,
And let their hearts be open to the night,
Lying on "the bean", they hold each other near,
In a serene and tranquil, perfect sight.
Oh Victoria and Johnathan, may your love,
Withstand the test of time, and soar above.
